- In the fourth quarter of a game between the Golden State Warriors and the Chicago Bulls, Steph Curry suffered an ankle injury.
- Multiple news sources reported that the injury was feared to be serious and that Curry had to leave the game.
- However, MRI results came back clean and showed that the injury was not long-term.
- Curry at the time was averaging 26.9 points, 4.9 assists, and 4.4 rebounds while shooting 40.7 percent from three-point range in 59 games played for Golden State.
- The Warriors players reacted to Curry's injury, stating that they were optimistic that it was not serious and that he would recover soon.
